// 01 What are Cookies

In the PalBreed Tactical Terminal environment, cookies are small data packets transmitted between our server and your local node (browser). These packets are essential for Terminal Session Persistence, allowing the system to recognize your clearance level, preferred configuration settings, and calculation history.

Without these data packets, the terminal would lose your operational state every time a page refresh or node transition occurs.

// 02 How we use Cookies

bolt

Optimization

Reducing data load times by caching common static variables and UI state directly on your local node.

settings

Session State

Maintaining active filters in the Breeding Calculator and keeping your search parameters locked during navigation.

// 04 Managing Cookies

Operators have full control over their local data nodes. You may intercept, block, or purge cookies via your browser's security console. Note that restricting Necessary cookies will lead to critical terminal failures in the Breeding Calculator.

Chrome Settings > Privacy Firefox Options > Security Safari Preferences > Privacy

// 05 Third-Party Nodes

We deploy high-level analytics nodes from partners like Google and infrastructure providers like Cloudflare to ensure terminal uptime and performance. These nodes may deploy their own protocol cookies which are governed by their respective tactical privacy frameworks.
